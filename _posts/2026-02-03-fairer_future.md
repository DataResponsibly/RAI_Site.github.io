On **February 3**, NYU and CUNY convened Building a Fairer Future for NYC, a conversation bringing together leaders from academia, industry, and government to explore how technology and innovation can support a safer and more equitable city.
R/AI faculty participated actively in the program. **<a href="https://engineering.nyu.edu/faculty/julia-stoyanovich">Julia Stoyanovich</a>**, Director of the NYU Center for Responsible AI, delivered a keynote and joined the New York AI Exchange panel, while **<a href="https://engineering.nyu.edu/faculty/emily-black">Emily Black</a>** a faculty member of the Center, took part in the discussion *“Does Progress in AI Lead to NYC’s Economic Development?”*

In her keynote, *“Responsible AI Beyond the Principles: Building a Regime of Distributed Accountability,”* Prof. Stoyanovich discussed how responsibility in AI systems is shared across designers, deployers, policymakers, and institutions. Drawing on real-world examples from city technologies, she emphasized that effective governance requires transparency, education, and clear policy frameworks to ensure that AI systems support the public interest.
